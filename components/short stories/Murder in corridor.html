Murder in Corridor

One of the stereotypes or beliefs among hostellers is the ghost stories happening in the hostel. In every hostel, even though nothing serious or scary happens, rumours among the hostellers spread easily. This is the story of a girls’ hostel located near an old building where no one lives. The girls in the hostel are crazy, always making fun of others or scaring them. Meena, a new girl in the hostel, is staying with her roommates, who have already been there for a while. Meena has much longer hair than the other girls in the hostel. Her roommates often make fun of her, so she mostly spends her time in the church, praying to God. Her roommates are jealous of her long hair. One day, they told Meena a ghost story.

They said that a girl who stayed in the hostel four years ago did not have long hair and desperately wanted thick, long hair. But no matter what she did, her hair wouldn’t grow. Her obsession turned into hatred toward anyone with long hair. Around the same time, a new warden with beautiful long hair was appointed to the hostel. The girl decided to ruin the warden’s hair by tampering with her shampoo and oil, but nothing happened. Eventually, the girl turned evil and mixed acid into the warden’s shampoo. However, when the warden tried to open the bottle, it slipped from her hand and spilled on the floor. The girl with short hair, watching from the corner, realized she couldn’t harm the warden’s hair that way. Instead, she plotted to kill her. That night, she attacked the warden, using her hair to strangle her and setting her hair on fire. The next morning, the warden’s charred body was discovered, and the girl feigned innocence with a creepy smile. The warden’s burnt body seemed to stare at her, but no one suspected her initially.

Shortly after, a new girl moved into the hostel as the girl’s roommate. This new girl had long, thick hair, which made her the next target. The girl killed her the same way she had killed the warden. Over time, the girl became a serial killer, murdering anyone with long hair. Her creepy smile started to raise suspicions among other hostelers. One night, while trying to kill her next target, a girl who was awake and talking to her boyfriend caught her in the act. With the help of other hostelers, they tied the killer to a window. By then, she had killed more than 10 girls and the warden. When the police arrived and untied her, she ran to the corridor, set herself on fire, and died, yelling her final words: "I’ll never leave anyone alive with long hair!"

After hearing this, Meena asked her roommates, "This is okay, but it happened years ago. Do you still believe it?" They replied, "Yes, of course! Her favourite number was 5, and this is the fifth year after her death. Our room number is 5, and your cot is the fifth one. Be careful! And if you have long hair, don’t go near the horror corridor, or her ghost will burn your hair!" Meena became scared because she valued her hair more than gold or cosmetics.

One midnight, Meena’s roommates planned to scare her. One girl pretended to be afraid of the dark and woke Meena up to fetch water. Meena agreed and went alone. She got stuck in fear when she realized that the usual way to the water cans was closed, leaving her with no choice but to use the horror corridor.

Her roommates watched her from a window, laughing at her prayers as she bravely stepped into the corridor. Suddenly, a black shadow with a handful of hair in one hand and a matchbox in the other appeared—the creepy girl’s ghost. Meena kept praying without stopping, even as the ghost approached her. Her roommates, terrified by the sight, were killed by the ghost one by one. The ghost then turned to Meena, who was trembling but praying fervently. As the ghost tried to attack, a white shadow emerged, protecting Meena. The creepy girl’s ghost couldn’t harm her and eventually disappeared with a terrifying sound.

Finally, as is often the case, the good spirit helped the girl and destroyed the evil spirit. This story reminds us that we all live with the hope of good spirits. A spirit is not only a godly entity but also positivity and energy.
