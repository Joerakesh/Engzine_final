THE CORRIDOR: A PASSAGE TO CONNECTION

Corridors are often overlooked spaces, merely transitional areas between destinations. However, they hold significance beyond their functional purpose. The corridor, a seemingly mundane passageway, serves as a vital artery connecting people, places, and experiences. Corridors, often overlooked, serve as vital connectors between spaces, people, and experiences. Beyond their functional purpose, they hold architectural, social, emotional, cultural, and psychological significance.

Architectural Significance:

Architecturally, it provides structural support, facilitates navigation, separates spaces while uniting them and creates a sense of continuity. It creates a showcase aesthetic appeal through design, lighting, and artwork. It also incorporates safety features like fire exits and CCTV surveillance.

Social Appearance:

In social appearance, the corridors facilitate social interactions, foster chance encounters, host informal gatherings, and witness shared memories, informal learning, and collaboration. They also serve as spaces for event hosting, from exhibitions to performances.

Emotional Memories:

The corridors evoke emotions. It's nostalgia from memories that are made.

Cultural Significance:

In cultural significance, the corridor symbolizes growth, represents the journey, embodies community spirit, and displays historical moments.

Psychological Impact:

In psychological impact, the corridor influences the mood through lighting, colour, and ambiance, as well as wayfinding and orientation through cognitive mapping. It will reduce stress through natural elements and provide a sense of control through navigation choices. The corridor, often underappreciated, plays a vital role in connecting people, spaces, and experiences. Its significance extends beyond physical purpose, embracing social, emotional, and cultural dimensions.
